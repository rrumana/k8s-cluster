# OpenSearch and app-replicated storage audit

Audit captured on 2026-07-21 around 20:45 PDT. This is a deferred follow-up
plan, not authorization to change the live logging stack. No OpenSearch index,
PVC, RBD image, collector, retention policy, or Ceph setting was changed during
the audit.

Post-migration status (2026-07-22): the block and CephFS migrations are
complete, the old `ceph-block` pool and legacy `ceph-filesystem` have been
deleted, and the retained cluster is active+clean. The remediation remains
deferred because the pre-existing `osd.0` BlueStore warning is still present
and the point-in-time usage, index, and collector measurements below must be
refreshed before any logging change.

Do not begin the remediation phases until the active CephFS media and Immich
transfers have completed, their verification and cutover gates have passed, and
Ceph is healthy without the current `osd.0` BlueStore slow-operation warning.

## Executive conclusion

The nominal PVC sizes overstate consumption because RBD is thin-provisioned,
but the underlying concern is valid. OpenSearch is presently a mostly
write-only Kubernetes log archive with unbounded retention, substantial noisy
self-ingestion, four effective copies of each primary log segment, and no
mechanism to return deleted ext4 blocks to Ceph.

The three 400 GiB OpenSearch claims are ceilings, not 1.2 TiB of reserved
physical space. They contain about 424.9 GiB of live filesystem data. The more
important findings are:

- `logs-*` contains 108 daily indices, 630.5 million documents, and every log
  since the stack was installed on 2026-04-06.
- OpenSearch has no ISM policies, no managed log indices, and no `logs-*` index
  template. Current retention is accidental and infinite.
- The 423.9 GiB OpenSearch store includes one application replica. Its unique
  primary data is 212.0 GiB. The size-2 Ceph pool then duplicates both copies,
  making the live index segments cost about 847.9 GiB raw.
- OpenSearch currently serves centralized pod stdout/stderr and ad-hoc Grafana
  Explore queries only. OpenSearch Dashboards is disabled, no provisioned
  dashboard queries the logs, and there are no OpenSearch alerting monitors.
  Search activity was negligible compared with indexing, and audit queries
  accounted for a meaningful part of the observed searches.
- Fluent Bit collects its own routine successful-delivery messages. In recent
  samples it produced about 39% of all documents, including repeated `200 OK`
  and `HTTP status=200` messages generated by the pipeline it feeds.
- At audit time all three Eva Fluent Bit pods reported Ready but had emitted no
  new output since the earlier DNS/connectivity incident. The control-node
  collectors were active while the Eva OSDs were producing logs, so the
  archive was incomplete despite its cost.

## Whole-pool accounting

`ceph-block-app-replicated` has `size: 2`. It contained 43 PVC images plus two
Valkey CSI snapshot images at audit time.

| Consumer | PVC ceiling | Mounted filesystem used |
| --- | ---: | ---: |
| OpenSearch | 1,200 GiB | 424.86 GiB |
| PostgreSQL `media` | 390 GiB | 4.404 GiB |
| PostgreSQL `productivity` | 240 GiB | 2.003 GiB |
| PostgreSQL `platform` | 240 GiB | 1.832 GiB |
| PostgreSQL `other` | 240 GiB | 1.805 GiB |
| PostgreSQL `ai` | 240 GiB | 0.694 GiB |
| Valkey live volumes | 60 GiB | 0.061 GiB |
| Valkey local backup artifacts | 42 GiB | unmounted/COW snapshots |
| Vault | 30 GiB | 0.048 GiB |
| **Total** | **2,682 GiB** | **435.7 GiB mounted** |

The 2,682 GiB total is thin-provisioned address space. It is not reserved raw
capacity. Conversely, mounted filesystem usage is not a complete measure of
Ceph allocation because blocks deleted inside ext4 remain allocated in RBD
until the filesystem issues discard/TRIM.

Ceph's exact pool-level accounting was:

- 1,019.5 GiB logical stored.
- 2,039.0 GiB raw after size-2 replication.
- About 9.2% of the cluster's then-current raw consumption.

Only 435.7 GiB was visible as live files on mounted PVCs. The approximately
583.8 GiB logical gap costs about 1.14 TiB raw. The likely dominant cause is
allocated-but-now-free RBD extents left by block migrations, OpenSearch shard
recovery, Lucene segment merges, and deletions. The small unmounted Valkey
artifacts and RBD metadata cannot explain a gap of this size.

All audited images exposed only the RBD `layering` feature, without
`object-map` or `fast-diff`. A pool-wide `rbd du` would therefore scan roughly
266,000 RADOS objects. That scan was deliberately deferred while migrations
and the `osd.0` warning were active. There was no RBD trash in the pool.

## Logging data and retention

The pipeline is:

`/var/log/containers/*.log` -> Fluent Bit on every node -> Data Prepper ->
daily `logs-YYYY.MM.DD` indices -> Grafana's `logs-*` datasource.

It does not currently store host journald, Kubernetes API audit files, traces,
OTel data, or application search content. Other application search services
are separate from this cluster.

At the audit snapshot:

- `logs-*`: 108 indices, 630.5 million documents, 423.9 GiB including the
  OpenSearch replica, and 212.0 GiB of primary segments.
- `security-auditlog-*`: 108 tiny daily indices, 3,260 documents, and about
  32 MiB total.
- The cluster had roughly 462 shards. The tiny security audit indices accounted
  for 216 of them, nearly half the shard count for negligible stored data.
- Recent complete days averaged about 4.53 GiB/day of primary segments,
  9.06 GiB/day after the OpenSearch replica, and 18.12 GiB/day raw after Ceph
  replication.

Observed index deletion estimates at the snapshot were:

| Retention window | OpenSearch store retained | Approximate Ceph raw retained | Approximate raw reclaimed |
| --- | ---: | ---: | ---: |
| 14 days | 112 GiB | 225 GiB | 623 GiB |
| 30 days | 162 GiB | 324 GiB | 524 GiB |
| 60 days | 280 GiB | 559 GiB | 289 GiB |

Those rows describe the indices that existed during the audit. If the recent
higher ingestion rate continues unchanged, a steady 30-day window would trend
toward about 272 GiB in OpenSearch and 544 GiB raw; a 60-day window would trend
toward about 544 GiB in OpenSearch and 1.06 TiB raw. Filtering the self-ingested
success chatter should materially lower both projections.

## Ingestion quality findings

The current Fluent Bit input tails every Kubernetes container log without a
source or severity exclusion. `Log_Level info` also makes each collector report
routine HTTP output success. Fluent Bit then tails those messages and submits
them through the same output, creating a direct self-ingestion loop.

Representative recent document shares were:

- Fluent Bit itself: about 39%.
- Ceph mon and mgr: about 18.5%.
- Argo CD controllers and repo-server: about 13%.

Data Prepper and OpenSearch logs are also re-ingested. During DNS, network, or
backpressure incidents, their retries and stack traces amplify load on the same
pipeline that is already impaired.

Delivery is at-least-once. Fluent Bit has a persistent tail-position database
and infinite HTTP retry, but Data Prepper assigns no deterministic document ID.
An ambiguous retry can therefore duplicate a document. Data Prepper sets
`@timestamp` from receive time rather than event time, although the original
event time remains in the document.

There is no deliberate log mapping template. Dynamic mapping indexes many
metadata fields as both analyzed text and keyword, including identifiers that
only need keyword search. It also gives full messages a largely unnecessary
keyword representation. The indices use the default codec rather than
`best_compression`.

The Eva collector failure needs diagnosis before judging the pipeline healthy:

- All three pods remained Ready.
- Their last stdout activity was around 09:16 PDT during the connectivity
  incident, with no new collector lines in a later ten-minute sample.
- The control-node collectors remained active.
- Eva OSD containers were demonstrably generating new logs.
- The collectors had large retry counters, only a 50 MB in-memory input buffer,
  infinite output retry, and no filesystem buffering configuration.

## Deferred remediation sequence

### Gate 0: finish storage migration

Require all of the following before changing logging or reclaim behavior:

- Media and Immich transfers, verification, and required cutovers are complete.
- No migration or final-delta writer is active.
- All OSDs are up and in, all PGs are active+clean, both filesystems are
  healthy, and Ceph reports no slow operations.
- `osd.0` is no longer producing BlueStore commit stalls.
- Current pool usage, OpenSearch health, node allocation, index list, and
  Fluent Bit counters have been captured again.

### Phase 1: restore complete collection

Diagnose why the Eva Fluent Bit instances remained Ready while their input and
output stopped. Correct the persistent configuration through GitOps and roll
collectors in a controlled manner. Confirm every node advances input and output
counters, catches up without drops or an uncontrolled retry storm, and reports
fresh logs in OpenSearch.

Consider filesystem buffering and meaningful readiness checks so a collector
cannot remain Ready indefinitely with a stalled tail/output pipeline.

### Phase 2: stop avoidable ingestion

- Stop routine Fluent Bit HTTP success messages, while retaining actionable
  warnings and errors.
- Prevent the collector from feeding its own success chatter back into the
  pipeline.
- Evaluate severity-aware filtering for Data Prepper, OpenSearch, Ceph, Argo,
  and other high-volume sources. Do not blanket-drop incident diagnostics.
- Preserve event time and assess deterministic document IDs if duplicate
  ingestion is measurable.

Measure document rate and source shares for several days before setting final
capacity expectations.

### Phase 3: implement bounded retention and mappings

The initial recommendation is 30 days for ordinary cluster logs unless a
specific incident-forensics requirement justifies 60 or 90 days. Implement an
idempotent, GitOps-managed ISM policy and automatic attachment mechanism for
new and existing `logs-*` indices. Prove the policy is attached before deleting
any old index.

Give `security-auditlog-*` and query-insight indices explicit lifecycle rules.
Reduce the tiny audit-index shard overhead, for example by using a longer index
period or a suitable retention policy.

Add an index template for new log indices:

- Stable identifiers and Kubernetes metadata should generally be keyword-only.
- `message` should generally be analyzed text without an unbounded keyword
  duplicate.
- Adopt `best_compression` only after measuring its CPU/storage tradeoff.
- Preserve fields required by Grafana Explore and any future alerts.

Delete expired indices through OpenSearch lifecycle management, never by
deleting RBD objects or PVC contents.

### Phase 4: reclaim freed RBD extents

Deleting indices frees ext4 blocks but may not immediately reduce Ceph pool
usage. Once OpenSearch is green after retention cleanup, evaluate a controlled
`fstrim` campaign one PVC/node at a time.

Before each trim:

- Require Ceph HEALTH_OK and no recovery/backfill.
- Record pool bytes, OSD latency, and the target filesystem usage.
- Keep the other OpenSearch nodes healthy and available.
- Monitor client IO, OSD latency, slow operations, and OpenSearch allocation.
- Stop and investigate on material latency or health degradation.

Do not enable continuous `discard` cluster-wide without a separate performance
test. After the quiet migration window, a pool-wide or per-image `rbd du` scan
can establish exact image allocation if the diagnostic load is acceptable.

### Phase 5: reassess capacity and replicas

Keep the existing 400 GiB PVCs initially. Their unused capacity is thin
headroom and shrinking is unsupported in place; recreating smaller claims would
not itself reclaim the stale allocated extents.

After at least one complete retention window, reconsider PVC size only if the
steady-state data justifies a rolling replacement node pool. Separately review
whether an OpenSearch replica remains worth the availability benefit. Retain
one replica initially: filtering, bounded retention, and trim offer much safer
savings than removing shard-level HA during the first cleanup.

## Success criteria

- Every node collector continuously advances input/output counters and no
  collector can be Ready while stalled.
- Routine Fluent Bit success chatter is no longer a leading log source.
- All matching indices have a visible lifecycle policy and no ordinary log is
  older than the chosen retention window.
- OpenSearch remains green throughout cleanup, with balanced shards and ample
  low-watermark headroom.
- Security audit indices no longer consume hundreds of shards for a few MiB.
- Ceph pool `STORED` and raw `USED` decline after controlled trim, without slow
  operations or PG degradation.
- Grafana log exploration still works for representative namespaces, pods,
  severity levels, and time ranges.
